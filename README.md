# Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug (1914–2009) was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate whose work is credited with saving over a billion lives. Known as the _“Father of the Green Revolution,”_ Borlaug developed high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties and promoted modern agricultural practices that transformed food production across Asia and Latin America.

## Contributions

- Developed semi-dwarf, high-yield wheat varieties.
- Helped dramatically increase crop production in countries facing famine.
- Advocated for scientific innovation to combat global hunger.
- Founded the World Food Prize to honor advancements in agriculture and food security.

## Legacy

Dr. Borlaug’s work reshaped global agriculture and remains a cornerstone in efforts to fight hunger. His dedication to science, education, and humanitarianism continues to inspire researchers, policymakers, and farmers around the world.
